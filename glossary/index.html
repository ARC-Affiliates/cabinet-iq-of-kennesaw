Kitchen Cabinet Design Glossary

Definitions of Kitchen Cabinet Design terms as used by Cabinet IQ Of Kennesaw in Kennesaw, GA. 22 terms defined.

Jump to: C · D · F · I · K · L · M · P · Q · S · T

C

Cabinet Box

The cabinet box, or carcass, is the structural shell of a cabinet that holds shelves, drawers, and doors. It's typically constructed from plywood, particleboard, or MDF, with plywood considered the highest quality. The box's material and construction significantly impact a cabinet's durability and longevity.

Cabinet Lead Time

Cabinet lead time is the period between placing an order and receiving the finished cabinets ready for installation. Stock cabinets may ship in days, while semi-custom takes weeks and fully custom cabinets can require eight to twelve weeks or more. Understanding lead time is essential for planning a kitchen remodel timeline.

Cabinet Refacing

Cabinet refacing involves replacing doors, drawer fronts, and visible cabinet surfaces while keeping the existing cabinet boxes in place. It offers a fresh look at a lower cost and shorter timeline than full replacement. Refacing works best when the existing cabinet structure is still in good condition.

Crown Molding

Crown molding is decorative trim installed along the top of wall cabinets to create a finished, polished transition to the ceiling. It adds architectural detail and can make cabinets appear taller and more custom. Styles range from simple cove profiles to elaborate stacked designs.

Custom Cabinetry

Custom cabinetry is built to exact specifications based on a homeowner's kitchen dimensions, storage needs, and style preferences. Unlike stock or semi-custom options, every aspect including size, materials, finish, and hardware is selected individually. This approach maximizes space efficiency and design personalization.

D

Door Reveal

Door reveal refers to the visible gap between cabinet doors and drawer fronts when they're closed. Consistent, narrow reveals indicate quality craftsmanship and precise installation. Different overlay styles produce different reveal widths, affecting the overall appearance of the cabinetry.

Dovetail Drawers

Dovetail drawers feature interlocking wedge-shaped joints where the drawer sides meet the front and back. This traditional joinery method creates exceptionally strong, durable drawers that resist pulling apart over time. Dovetail construction is a hallmark of well-built, quality cabinetry.

F

Face Frame Cabinets

Face frame cabinets feature a wooden frame attached to the front of the cabinet box, providing structural support and a traditional look. The frame creates mounting points for doors and drawers and is common in American-style kitchens. They allow for partial, full, or inset door styles.

Frameless Cabinets

Frameless cabinets, also called European-style cabinets, are constructed without a face frame, with doors attached directly to the cabinet box. This design offers more interior storage space and a sleek, modern appearance. They typically feature full-overlay doors that cover the entire cabinet front.

Full Overlay

Full overlay refers to cabinet doors and drawer fronts that cover nearly the entire face of the cabinet box, leaving only a small reveal between them. This style offers a contemporary, seamless look while maximizing the visible door surface. It works with both framed and frameless construction.

I

Inset Cabinets

Inset cabinets have doors and drawer fronts that sit flush inside the cabinet face frame rather than overlapping it. This precision style delivers a timeless, furniture-like appearance often found in high-end and classic kitchens. They require expert craftsmanship due to tight tolerances and typically cost more than overlay options.

K

Kitchen Work Triangle

The kitchen work triangle is a design principle connecting the sink, stove, and refrigerator in an efficient layout. Each leg should measure between four and nine feet, with the total perimeter between thirteen and twenty-six feet. Following this guideline reduces unnecessary movement during meal preparation.

L

Lazy Susan

A lazy susan is a rotating shelf system installed inside corner cabinets to provide accessible storage in otherwise hard-to-reach spaces. It typically features round or kidney-shaped shelves that spin on a central axis. This solution maximizes corner storage efficiency in kitchen layouts.

M

MDF Cabinets

MDF, or medium-density fiberboard, is an engineered wood product used for cabinet doors and panels, especially those intended to be painted. It provides a smooth, stable surface that resists warping and cracking better than solid wood in painted applications. MDF is commonly used in shaker-style painted doors.

P

Pull-Out Pantry

A pull-out pantry is a tall, narrow cabinet with shelves or baskets that slide out for full access to stored items. It maximizes vertical storage in small spaces and keeps contents visible and reachable. This feature is popular in modern kitchen designs focused on organization and efficiency.

Q

Quartersawn Wood

Quartersawn wood is cut at a specific angle that produces straight, vertical grain patterns and exceptional dimensional stability. This cutting method reduces warping and shrinking over time, making it ideal for high-quality cabinet doors. Quartersawn oak is particularly prized in Craftsman and Mission-style cabinetry.

S

Semi-Custom Cabinets

Semi-custom cabinets offer pre-designed sizes and styles with limited modifications, such as adjustments to depth, width, or finish. They strike a balance between affordable stock cabinets and fully custom builds. Lead times are generally shorter than custom while still providing meaningful personalization.

Shaker Style

Shaker style cabinets feature a clean five-piece door design with a recessed center panel and simple, square edges. Originating from Shaker furniture traditions, the style bridges traditional and modern aesthetics. Its versatility makes it one of the most popular cabinet door choices for kitchen remodels.

Soft-Close Hinges

Soft-close hinges use a hydraulic mechanism to gently pull cabinet doors closed without slamming. This feature reduces wear on the cabinet, minimizes noise, and extends the life of the doors and frame. Most quality cabinet lines now include soft-close hinges as a standard feature.

Stock Cabinets

Stock cabinets are mass-produced in standard sizes and finishes, available for quick purchase and installation. They offer the most budget-friendly option but provide limited choices in style, materials, and dimensions. Stock cabinets work well for straightforward layouts where customization isn't a priority.

T

Thermofoil

Thermofoil is a vinyl finish heat-pressed onto an MDF substrate to create a smooth, seamless cabinet door surface. It offers a uniform appearance, easy cleaning, and resistance to chipping and moisture. Thermofoil is typically more affordable than painted wood while providing a clean, modern look.

Toe Kick

The toe kick is the recessed space at the bottom of base cabinets that allows people to stand closer to the counter comfortably. It typically measures about three to four inches deep and three inches tall. The toe kick can also house heating vents or hidden storage drawers.
