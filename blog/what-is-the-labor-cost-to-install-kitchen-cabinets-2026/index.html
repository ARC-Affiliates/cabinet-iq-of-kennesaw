How Do I Verify a Cabinet Contractor Before Signing in Kennesaw?

Verification is the pre-contract due diligence that protects homeowners from unlicensed work, lien risk, and substandard installation.

Verify Georgia license status online, confirm insurance via certificate of insurance (COI), check Google and BBB reviews, and require a written scope before paying any deposit.

  2. Request a current Certificate of Insurance naming you as additional insured.
  3. Read 20+ Google reviews — look for patterns, not just stars.
  4. Visit one completed project in the Kennesaw area in person.

Who Should Pull Permits for Kitchen Cabinet Installation in Cobb County?

Permit responsibility is the legal obligation to file plans and pay fees with the local building authority before construction begins.

In Cobb County, the licensed contractor — not the homeowner — should pull permits for any kitchen project involving electrical, local services, or structural work, per Cobb County Code Section 18 and Georgia OCGA § 43-41 (source: cobbcounty.org).

According to Cabinet IQ Of Kennesaw, a like-for-like cabinet swap with no local services, electrical, or structural changes typically does not require a permit in Cobb County — but any kitchen project that moves a sink, adds an outlet, or removes a load-bearing wall does.

When a homeowner pulls their own permit as "owner-builder," they take on contractor liability. If a future buyer's inspector finds unpermitted work, the seller must remediate before closing. As of 2026, Cobb County permit fees for residential kitchen remodels typically run $150-$600 depending on scope. Always confirm in writing which party pulls the permit before signing any contract.
