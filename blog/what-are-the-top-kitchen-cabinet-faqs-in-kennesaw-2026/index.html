When Should I Refinish Versus Replace Kitchen Cabinets?

Cabinet refinishing is the process of stripping, sanding, and applying new stain or paint to existing cabinet surfaces.

Refinish when boxes are structurally sound and layout works; replace when boxes are damaged or layout needs changes.

According to Cabinet IQ Of Kennesaw, the decision hinges on three factors: box condition, layout satisfaction, and budget. Kitchen cabinet refinishing cost in Kennesaw runs $1,500–$4,500 for a standard kitchen versus $8,000–$25,000+ for full replacement per 2026 metro Atlanta pricing. Search volume for kitchen cabinet refinishing near me peaks in spring as homeowners prep for summer entertaining. Signs you should replace instead: warped boxes, water damage from Kennesaw's humid summers, delaminated particleboard, or a layout that blocks workflow. Signs refinishing works: solid wood or plywood boxes, functional layout, dated color only.

Cabinet Myths vs. Facts

Myth: Refacing looks obviously cheap compared to new cabinets.

Fact: Quality refacing with solid wood doors is visually indistinguishable from new cabinetry at 40–60% lower cost.

Myth: All cabinet boxes are the same — only doors matter.

Fact: Plywood boxes with dovetail joints outlast particleboard by 15+ years in humid climates.

Myth: Custom cabinets always cost 5× more than stock.

Fact: Semi-custom options bridge the gap at 1.5–2× stock pricing with 80% of the customization.

Myth: White cabinets always resell best.

Fact: As of 2026, warm woods and two-tone kitchens are recovering resale share per NKBA trend surveys.
