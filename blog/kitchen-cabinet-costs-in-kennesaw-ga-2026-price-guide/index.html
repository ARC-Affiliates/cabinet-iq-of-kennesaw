What Drives Kitchen Cabinet Pricing in Kennesaw?

Kitchen cabinet pricing is the total cost of cabinet boxes, doors, hardware, finishing, delivery, and labor for a given kitchen footprint.

Material grade, box construction, door style, and linear footage drive 80% of the final price.

The biggest cost lever is the cabinet box (the structural shell that holds shelves and drawers). Plywood boxes cost 20–40% more than particleboard but resist warping in Georgia's humid summers. Door style matters too — a flat-panel slab door is cheaper to mill than a five-piece Shaker or raised-panel design. Finishing adds another tier: factory-sprayed conversion varnish costs more than a stained finish but lasts longer under daily use.

For a typical 10x12 Kennesaw kitchen in 2026, homeowners should budget $6,000 to $26,000 installed, with semi-custom cabinets at $12,000 to $18,000 covering most middle-market remodels.

Stock vs. Custom: Which Wins?

Stock vs. custom: stock is the better value when your kitchen has standard wall lengths and no awkward corners, because you avoid premium milling fees. Custom is worth the spend when ceilings exceed 9 feet, walls are out of plumb, or you need integrated appliance panels — situations where stock boxes leave dead space and require fillers.

When Do You Need Permits for Kitchen Cabinet Work in Kennesaw?

A permit is a written authorization from the local building department to perform regulated construction work.

Cabinet swaps alone do not require a permit, but local services, electrical, or gas changes do.

Under City of Kennesaw Code of Ordinances Chapter 18 (Buildings and Building Regulations) and Cobb County's adoption of the 2018 International Residential Code, replacing cabinets in the same footprint is exempt. Moving a sink, adding under-cabinet lighting circuits, or relocating a gas range triggers a permit through the Cobb County Development & Inspections Division (source: cobbcounty.org). As of 2026, expect $75 to $250 in combined permit fees for a typical kitchen with one local services and one electrical change.

What Credentials Should Kennesaw Kitchen Designers Have?

Credentials are licenses, insurance policies, and trade certifications that verify a contractor is qualified and accountable.

In Georgia, look for a state residential contractor license, general liability insurance of at least $500,000, and NKBA membership.

  • Georgia Residential Contractor License — required for jobs over $2,500 per O.C.G.A. § 43-41 (verify at sos.ga.gov).
  • General Liability Insurance — industry minimum $500,000; $1 million is standard for kitchen scope.
  • Workers' Compensation — required in Georgia for contractors with three or more employees.
  • NKBA Certification — the National Kitchen & Bath Association offers AKBD and CKBD credentials (verify at nkba.org).
  • Manufacturer Certifications — KCMA Environmental Stewardship Program certification indicates compliant cabinet construction (source: kcma.org).

Cabinet Pricing Myths vs. Facts

Myth: Custom cabinets always cost twice as much as semi-custom.

Fact: The gap is 40–80% on average; standardized custom shops in Georgia have narrowed it.

Myth: Refacing is always cheaper than replacement.

Fact: Refacing saves only when existing boxes are structurally sound; otherwise replacement is more durable.

Myth: Soft-close hinges are a luxury upgrade.

Fact: As of 2026, soft-close is standard on most semi-custom lines at no added cost.

Myth: All plywood boxes are equal.

Fact: Furniture-grade 3/4" plywood with dadoed joints outperforms 1/2" stapled plywood.
