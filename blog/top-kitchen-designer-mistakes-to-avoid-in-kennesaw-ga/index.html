Why Do Most Kitchen Design Projects Go Over Budget in Cobb County?

Budget overruns in kitchen design are cost increases above the signed contract caused by scope changes, undocumented decisions, or rework.

Most Cobb County kitchen projects exceed budget because scope is defined in conversation, not in writing — then change orders accumulate.

A change order (a written contract amendment that modifies price or scope after work begins) typically adds 5-12% per occurrence. Three change orders is the average, which is why the National Association of Home Builders reports kitchen remodels finish 18-22% over initial budget (source: nahb.org).

In Kennesaw specifically, the issue often stems from designers skipping a measured site visit. Homes near Swift-Cantrell Park and the historic Kennesaw Mountain district frequently have non-square walls, settled foundations, and 1970s-era local services offsets that affect cabinet runs.

Three drivers of overruns

  • Vague allowances — "countertop allowance: $4,000" with no slab selected
  • Verbal upgrades — soft-close hinges or pull-outs added mid-build
  • Demo surprises — knob-and-tube wiring, water damage, or out-of-code venting
"The kitchen should be planned around how the household actually cooks, stores, and cleans — not around a stock cabinet catalog."— National Kitchen and Bath Association, nkba.org

How Do You Verify a Kitchen Designer Is Properly Credentialed?

Credential verification is the process of confirming a designer holds active licenses, insurance, and industry certifications before signing a contract.

Verify Georgia residential contractor licensing, request a certificate of insurance, and confirm any NKBA or AKBD credentials directly with the issuing body.

Kitchen Designer vs General Contractor: Which Do You Hire First?

A kitchen designer focuses on space planning, cabinetry, and aesthetics; a general contractor manages construction trades and permits.

Hire the kitchen designer first — they produce the drawings the contractor and inspector both need.

Kitchen designer vs general contractor: a kitchen designer is the right starting point because layout decisions drive electrical, local services, and local service rough-in locations. A general contractor is essential later because they hold the licenses to coordinate trades, pull permits under City of Kennesaw Code of Ordinances Chapter 14, and manage the build schedule. Skipping the designer means the contractor builds whatever fits — not what works.

7-point pre-hire verification checklist

  1. Confirm active Georgia residential contractor license number on the state website.
  2. Request a current certificate of insurance with you listed as additional insured.
  3. Ask for 3 completed Kennesaw, Woodstock, or Marietta projects from the last 24 months.
  4. Require a measured field survey before any quote is finalized.
  5. Demand a 3D rendering — not just a 2D floor plan — before signing.
  6. Get an itemized cabinet specification (line, door style, finish, construction).
  7. Review the written change-order policy and payment schedule.

The single best protection against budget overruns when hiring kitchen designers in Kennesaw is requiring a 3D rendering and itemized cabinet specification before the contract is signed — not after.

Myths vs facts

Myth: All-wood cabinets are always better than plywood-box cabinets.

Fact: Furniture-grade plywood boxes resist humidity better than solid wood in Georgia's climate and are the NKBA-preferred substrate.

Myth: A designer who quotes the lowest price will save you money.
