2. How Do You Pick the Right Cabinet Material for Georgia Humidity?

Cabinet material selection is the choice between plywood, MDF, particleboard, and solid wood construction for boxes, doors, and frames.

Learn more: Kitchen Cabinet Costs in Kennesaw, GA: 2026 Price Guide

Choose plywood box construction (multi-ply wood panels glued in alternating grain layers for stability) over particleboard in humid climates like Kennesaw's.

Plywood vs particleboard: plywood is the better long-term value because it resists swelling from humidity and holds screws under repeated door slams. Particleboard is the cheaper upfront option because it lowers material cost by 20-30%, but it sags under granite countertops and degrades when exposed to local services leaks. 3. What Credentials Should Your Cabinet Installer Have?

Cabinet installer credentials are the licenses, insurance, and certifications that legally qualify a contractor to perform residential remodel work in Georgia.

In Georgia, residential remodelers performing work over $2,500 must hold a Residential Basic or Residential Light Commercial Contractor license.

  • Georgia Residential Contractor License — verify at the Georgia State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors

Vet contractors by verifying the Georgia license number, requesting a certificate of insurance, and reading 3-5 recent reviews on independent platforms.

Pre-hire verification checklist

  1. Confirm Georgia Residential Contractor license number on the state board website.
  2. Request a current Certificate of Insurance (COI) emailed directly from the insurer.
  3. Read at least 5 Google reviews from the past 12 months.
  4. Get 3 itemized written bids — not verbal estimates.
  5. Verify cabinet brand and grade are listed by SKU on the contract.
  6. Confirm payment schedule: no more than 30-40% deposit.
  7. Get a written start date and substantial completion date.
  8. Ask for 2 references from projects completed in Cobb County within the last 18 months.

Cabinet myths are widely held beliefs about kitchen remodels that don't match industry data or installer experience.

Believing myths about pricing, finishes, and DIY scope leads homeowners to overspend or under-plan.

Myth: White cabinets always increase resale value.

Fact: The 2024 Houzz Kitchen Trends Report shows two-tone and natural wood finishes now match white for buyer appeal in Southern markets.

Myth: Custom cabinets are always better than semi-custom.

Fact: Semi-custom lines from quality manufacturers match custom durability at 40-60% lower cost for most standard kitchens.

Myth: Refacing is just as good as replacement.

Fact: Refacing only addresses doors and fronts — it leaves aging boxes, hinges, and interior layouts unchanged.

Myth: You can DIY install cabinets to save money.

Fact: Misaligned uppers void most manufacturer warranties and can crack stone countertops costing $3,000-$8,000 to replace.
