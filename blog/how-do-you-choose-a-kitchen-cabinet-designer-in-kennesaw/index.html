What's the Difference Between Semi-Custom and Custom Cabinets?

Semi-custom versus custom is a category distinction based on how much the cabinet boxes can be modified from a manufacturer's stock list.

Semi-custom cabinets allow size, finish, and accessory modifications within a fixed catalog; custom cabinets are built from scratch to any dimension.

Semi-custom vs custom: semi-custom is the practical pick for most Kennesaw homes because the 3-inch size increments cover 90% of real kitchen walls at 40–60% lower cost. Custom is the right tradeoff when ceilings exceed 9 feet, when angled walls require non-standard boxes, or when an exotic species like rift-sawn white oak is non-negotiable. According to Cabinet IQ Of Kennesaw, in roughly 7 of 10 projects within the Kennesaw 30144 ZIP, semi-custom delivers the visual outcome clients wanted from a custom quote.

When Should You Replace Kitchen Cabinets Instead of Refacing?

Cabinet replacement is the full removal and substitution of cabinet boxes and doors; refacing keeps boxes and changes only doors and veneer.

Replace cabinets when boxes are warped, water-damaged, or laid out poorly; reface when boxes are structurally sound and you only want a new look.

Why Do Kitchen Cabinet Projects Go Over Budget?

Budget overruns in cabinet projects are unplanned cost increases driven by scope changes, hidden conditions, or material upgrades mid-project.

The top three causes are mid-project door style changes, discovering local services or electrical that must move, and upgrading countertops after seeing samples.

Experts at Cabinet IQ Of Kennesaw recommend a 10–15% contingency line item on every Kennesaw kitchen project. Common scope creep includes:

  1. Adding soft-close hardware after seeing the showroom demo (+$300–$800)
  2. Upgrading from laminate to quartz countertops (+$2,000–$5,000)
  3. Relocating the sink to the island (+$1,500–$3,500 local services)
  4. Choosing a painted finish over stain (+8–15% on cabinet cost)
  5. Adding crown molding and light rail (+$400–$1,200)

Myths and facts about kitchen cabinets in Kennesaw

Myth: All cabinet boxes are made the same; only the door style matters.

Fact: Box construction varies widely. Plywood boxes with dovetailed drawer joinery outlast particleboard with stapled drawers by 10–15 years.

Myth: Buying cabinets from a big-box store is always cheaper.

Fact: Big-box stock prices look lower but exclude design time, accurate measuring, and installation — totals often land within 5–10% of a designer-dealer quote.

Myth: White cabinets always boost resale value.

Fact: The 2025 NKBA Design Trends Report shows natural wood tones and two-tone kitchens overtaking all-white in buyer preference.

Myth: Refacing lasts as long as new cabinets.

Fact: Refacing depends on the underlying boxes. If the boxes are 25 years old, the reface is only as durable as what's underneath.
