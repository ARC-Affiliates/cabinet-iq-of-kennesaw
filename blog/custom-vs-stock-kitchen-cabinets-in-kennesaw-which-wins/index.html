What Is the Difference Between Custom and Stock Cabinets?

Custom vs. stock cabinetry is the distinction between cabinets built to your exact specifications and pre-manufactured boxes in fixed sizes.

Stock cabinets come in standard 3-inch width increments and ship from warehouses; custom cabinets are built from scratch to your kitchen's exact measurements, finishes, and storage needs.

Stock cabinets are mass-produced in widths from 9 to 48 inches in 3-inch jumps, with limited door styles and 4–6 stain choices. Semi-custom cabinets (factory-built boxes with expanded size, finish, and accessory options) sit in the middle. True custom cabinets — built by shops or premium lines like those installed by Cabinet IQ Of Kennesaw — accommodate any dimension, wood species, inset construction, and built-in feature like spice pull-outs or appliance garages.

Construction Quality Differences

  • Box material: Stock often uses 1/2-inch particleboard; custom uses 3/4-inch plywood.
  • Joinery: Stock uses dowels and staples; custom uses dovetail drawers and mortise-and-tenon frames.
  • Finish: Stock uses thermofoil or basic stain; custom uses conversion varnish or catalyzed lacquer rated for 20+ years.

Myths vs. Facts

Myth: Custom cabinets always cost three times as much as stock.

Fact: Custom typically runs 2–4x stock pricing, but semi-custom closes that gap to 1.5–2x with similar visual results.

Myth: You can swap doors and call it "custom."

Fact: Refacing keeps the original box, which means original storage limitations and original moisture damage remain.

Myth: All plywood boxes are equal.

Fact: 3/4-inch 9-ply Baltic birch outperforms 1/2-inch 5-ply pine by a wide margin in humidity resistance.

Myth: Stock cabinets can't look high-end.

Fact: With quality hardware and proper installation, mid-tier stock lines photograph nearly identically to semi-custom in tract homes under $400,000.
